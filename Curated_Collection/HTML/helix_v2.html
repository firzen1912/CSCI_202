Assembly Step

The PCB kit already have all of the small component machine solder in to the main PCB. Then, I follow the step of this soldering guide authored by MakotoKurauchi and translated by N6UDP to fully solder the nice nano to the main PCB Note: Do not solder the RAW pin to main PCB.

For the construction of my firmware, I followed the detailed instructions provided in the ZMK guide documentation. This comprehensive guide from Kriscables served as a valuable resource, offering step-by-step insights into creating a firmware tailored to my requirements. By adhering to the guidelines outlined in the documentation, I successfully crafted a customized firmware that aligns seamlessly with the functionalities I envisioned for my keyboard.

To customize my keymap, I utilized the ZMK GUI keymap configurator for an intuitive and streamlined experience. This web-based tool allowed me to easily tailor my keyboard layout, define key functions, and configure layers, resulting in a personalized and efficient setup. Most of the function is enable on the my firmware repo version which can be clone and modified accordingly
